The problem

The solo agent ceiling

You've got Claude in one tab, Cursor in another, Copilot in your editor. Each one is impressive alone. But none of them coordinate.

Nobody's watching architecture while someone else ships features. Nobody remembers what was decided yesterday. Nobody pushes back on a bad approach.

You're the glue. You relay context, catch conflicts, maintain coherence across tools. It works — until it doesn't scale.

WIREFRAME: Plain text. No diagrams. The pain point should land through words alone.

The framework

A team, not a tool

Dax is an open-source framework for orchestrating AI agents as a structured software team. Each agent has a defined role — product manager, designer, architect, implementor, delivery coordinator — with its own context, decision rights, and persistent memory.

They communicate through a structured bridge, not ad-hoc prompting. You stay in the loop without being the loop.

Not just a pipeline

Pull roles into a room. Let them argue.

The delivery loop is the default mode. But the best decisions happen when roles with different perspectives sit at the same table.

Call a team meeting with any combination of roles. You pick who's in the room and what the question is. They bring their own context, their own biases, their own blind spots — and they push back on each other.

Creative alignment
Ziyal + Jake + Vic review a landing page draft. Does the design serve the copy? Does the copy match the brand?
3 roles, 3 perspectives, one room
Architecture risk
Dax + O'Brien + Odo debate whether a new integration is worth the attack surface. The architect, the builder, and the security analyst disagree productively.
Trade-offs surfaced before code is written
Sprint planning
Sisko + Kira + O'Brien scope the next slice. What's the bet? What's the smallest thing we can build to test it? What's actually feasible?
Discovery, delivery, and engineering aligned

You're always in the room. You set the agenda, you hear the arguments, you make the call. The roles give you perspectives you'd otherwise have to hold in your own head.
